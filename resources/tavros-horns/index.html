Some Lightweight Tavros Horns

So Tavros horns are always a pain and there are billions upon billions of tutorials on the internet so I’ll just add in. These were horns I had to make on the fly for a convention the very next day and they turned out pretty well. (I didn’t end up being used as a Tavros in the panel so no picture of me wearing them)

This will be text heavy.

So, materials:

  • Easy, bendable wire 
  • Masking tape
  • Toliet paper, paper towels or tissues
  • Paint and sealer
  • Patience 

So first, you’re going to make your wire into a 2-D shape of Tavros’s horns. 

And then you’re going to add in little circles around the entire 2-D shape until its kinda 3-D

And then you’re going to take your tissues/paper towels/toliet paper and shove it into the 3-D wire horn.

Once you have it properly stuffed, you’re going to take masking tape and completely cover the horn. If you’re lucky, which you probably won’t be, you can do with one layer. I’d suggest doing 3 layers of tape, to make sure its nice and smooth. 

Now this is important, do not wrap the bottom of the horns.

Now that you have it wrapped we’re going to address what to do with the bottom of the horns, add a bit of wire to the wire frame you made, this will be used to attach your horns to your wig. 

Now that you have the wire that will attach to the wefts of your wig, you can tape the ends until only the small bit of wire used to attach is showing. 

Now, the easy part, paint!

Once you have everything dried and shiny, you can work on attaching your horns to the wefts of your wig. Its a little tricky but, you should be able to get it as long as your horns aren’t heavy. 

And congratulations, you now have lightweight Tavros horns!
